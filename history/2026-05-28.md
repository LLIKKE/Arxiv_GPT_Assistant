## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs
The sheer volume of papers today underscores that reinforcement learning (RL) for LLM post-training has fully entered its "engineering maturity" phase, where the focus has shifted from proving the concept to optimizing every component of the pipeline. A dominant theme is the move beyond simplistic, trajectory-level rewards toward more granular and principled credit assignment. **GraphGPO**and**StepOPSD**directly tackle step-level attribution in agentic contexts, while**BASIS**and**RICE-PO**introduce critic-free methods that leverage batchwise or retrieval-based information to localize credit. Concurrently, the design of the reward signal itself is being refined:**Focal Reward**automatically balances multi-dimensional rubric-based rewards,**Tournament-GRPO**handles open-ended generation with group-wise comparisons, and**GeoFaith**jointly optimizes for outcome correctness and process faithfulness. Efficiency is also a key concern, with**Spend Your Rollouts Where It Counts**and**GAC**proposing adaptive allocation and noise-aware mixing for hybrid SFT-RL training. This collective work paints a picture of a field systematically dismantling the bottlenecks of RL for LLMs—credit assignment, reward sparsity, and training instability—to build more reliable and capable reasoning systems.

### Self-Evolving Agents & Agentic RL
The concept of autonomous self-improvement is no longer a vision but a rapidly maturing research direction, with multiple papers presenting concrete frameworks for creating agents that learn and adapt over time.**MiniMax-M2**and**SIA**propose end-to-end systems where agents iteratively refine their weights and behavior through feedback loops, while**MUSE-Autoskill**offers a more modular approach by formalizing the entire skill lifecycle—creation, memory, management, and evaluation—as a unified process. This is complemented by work on the data and training infrastructure for such systems:**AKBE**dynamically probes an agent's knowledge boundary to reduce wasteful tool calls, and**Verilog-Evolve**demonstrates how domain-specific agents can evolve their skills across sessions by maintaining histories. A critical insight from**NoisyAgent**and**MAIGO**is that robustness and history management are essential for real-world deployment, showing that self-evolution must account for environmental noise and conversational contamination. The field is clearly converging on the idea that the next leap in agent capability will come not from a single architectural innovation, but from the integration of closed-loop learning, sophisticated memory, and adaptive data pipelines.

### Latent Reasoning & Recurrent Architectures
A significant breakthrough is emerging in the design of models that can reason in a latent, token-free space, challenging the dominance of explicit chain-of-thought. The**STARS**and**Latent Recurrent Transformer**papers both propose novel architectures that reuse hidden states across tokens or loops, enabling implicit reasoning without generating intermediate text. This is a paradigm shift with profound implications for efficiency, as**Pair-In, Pair-Out**demonstrates by unifying input compression and output speculation through latent multi-token prediction. The application of this idea extends beyond language:**Latent Equation Embedding (LEE)**applies latent iterative refinement to symbolic regression, proving the concept's generality. However, this new capability also introduces new risks, as**Conceptual Steganography**warns that latent CoTs can hide malicious content that is robust to paraphrase-based defenses. The field is thus at a pivotal moment: latent reasoning promises massive gains in speed and scalability, but it demands new safety and interpretability mechanisms.

### Multimodal & Domain-Specific RL
Reinforcement learning is being aggressively applied to unlock new capabilities in multimodal systems, moving beyond standard language tasks.**Touch-R1**and**MedVol-R1**pioneer the use of GRPO with tactile-grounded and volumetric rewards, respectively, demonstrating that RL can effectively integrate non-linguistic signals into reasoning. For video understanding,**DynFrame**introduces a sophisticated RL training recipe with role-specific token-level advantages, while**InterSketch**uses interleaved visual-textual reasoning with self-correcting sketches. In the audio domain,**Learning When to Think While Listening** applies RL to optimize the timing of reasoning in large audio-language models. These works collectively show that the RL-for-LLM toolkit is highly transferable: the core challenges of reward design, credit assignment, and training stability are universal, but the specific implementations must be tailored to the unique structure of each modality. This cross-modal validation strengthens the argument that RL is a fundamental algorithmic primitive for building generally capable AI systems.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
