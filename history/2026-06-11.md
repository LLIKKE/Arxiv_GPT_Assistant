## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs
A major wave of innovation is targeting the fundamental limitations of Reinforcement Learning (RL) for Large Language Models, particularly in reasoning. Several works address the credit assignment problem in multi-step reasoning. Frameworks like **FlowTracer**and**3SPO**move beyond scalar rewards by tracing attention-induced information flow or using state scores to provide step-wise, token-level credit. A second critical trend is the integration of RL with latent reasoning, with**Dropout-GRPO**introducing structured stochasticity to enable Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) on continuous latent chains-of-thought. Furthermore, the field is actively addressing training stability and data efficiency:**CPPO**proposes a token-level trust region to mitigate cumulative prefix drift, while**TD-Grokking**and**TRACE**tackle sparse rewards and rollout budget inefficiency by decomposing problems and tree-structured allocation, respectively. Finally, critical vulnerabilities are being exposed, such as the ability of one-shot GRPO training to override alignment (**It Takes One to Bias Them All**) and the systematic alignment drift observed in reasoning models post-RL (**Does Reasoning Preserve Alignment?**).

### On-Policy Distillation and Self-Improvement
A dominant theme is the refinement of on-policy distillation, moving beyond simple imitation to more sophisticated, structurally aligned feedback mechanisms.**Anchored Residual On-Policy Distillation (AR-OPD)**and**SocraticPO**both leverage privileged teacher guidance during the student's own rollouts to mitigate hindsight bias, while**The Role of Feedback Alignment in Self-Distillation**explicitly demonstrates that step-aligned critique feedback is a key driver of effectiveness, outperforming binary rewards. This principle is applied across modalities, from bridging paralinguistic perception in speech models (**ParaBridge**) to using visual feedback for code generation (**Visual-SDPO**). The approach is also being extended to train specialized agents, as seen in pipelines that distill from incentive-aligned arena trajectories (**Bittensor Agent Arenas**) and in the**Cross-Modal Multi-Teacher On-Policy Distillation (MOPD)**for multimodal LLMs. A novel framework,**Role-Agent**, bootstraps this self-improvement by having a single LLM act as both agent and environment, using predicted state alignment as a process reward.

### Agentic RL and Exploration
Advances in agentic RL are centered on improving exploration and credit assignment in complex, multi-turn environments.**DiRL**introduces a direction-aware exploration strategy that uses model representations to distinguish reasoning from memorization, while**N-GRPO**fosters semantic diversity via embedding-level neighbor mixing. For practical agent deployment,**GUI-AC**enhances continual learning in GUI agents by using grounding certainty for adaptive advantage estimation. The challenge of sparse rewards in agentic search is addressed by**query recycling**for zero-variance queries, which improves training efficiency. A unified framework,**TRACE**, specifically targets the rollout budget allocation problem to enhance reward contrast in multi-turn agentic RL.

### Alignment and Safety
Research on alignment is expanding from post-training to data selection and defense. A novel**coupled task-reference selection**framework uses a minimax view to ensure safe fine-tuning, directly improving alignment via data curation. On the defense front, alignment-based methods like DPO and GRPO are shown to be effective against property inference attacks. A critical audit reveals that the RL and distillation processes used to create reasoning models can systematically degrade trustworthiness, causing alignment drift (**Does Reasoning Preserve Alignment?**). This underscores the need for robust safety evaluations throughout the RL pipeline.

### Multimodal and Specialized Architectures
For multimodal models, RL is being applied to optimize task-level objectives, as seen in the**ARM**autoregressive model which uses RL for cross-task synergy in generation and editing. A specialized application,**SD-GRPO**, tackles long-form vision-language generation by assigning per-segment advantages instead of a single scalar reward. In the speech domain,**Multi-Faceted Interactivity Alignment**uses axis-specific reward functions to optimize full-duplex speech model behavior. Finally, a new distillation framework,**PADD**, specifically addresses the challenge of training Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) students from dense teachers using path-refined policy optimization.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, scalable self-improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures with clear technical substance.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## 3. On-Policy Distillation for LLMs
We care about distillation methods that improve LLM capabilities by training on the model's own on-policy outputs, trajectories, preferences, corrections, or interaction data, especially when the student distribution is actively refreshed during training.
- **Strong match:** On-policy distillation, self-distillation from sampled rollouts, online teacher-student distillation, policy-improvement distillation, iterative distillation from model-generated reasoning traces, distillation from verifier/reward-selected on-policy samples, and methods that close the gap between training data distribution and the model's current behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new iterative distillation loop, sampling-and-selection strategy, online data refresh mechanism, teacher/student update schedule, or objective that measurably improves reasoning, instruction-following, alignment, tool use, or general LLM capability.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard offline knowledge distillation, static SFT on teacher-generated data, model compression only, data cleaning only, speculative decoding/distillation for speed only, or generic synthetic data generation without an on-policy or iterative policy-improvement component.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
