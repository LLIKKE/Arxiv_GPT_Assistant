💡 今日研究速览 (Daily Summary)

RL for LLMs

Today's papers reveal a significant maturation of RL-based post-training, moving beyond simple reward maximization to address fundamental methodological challenges. A clear trend is the focus on fixing known failure modes in group-relative policy optimization (GRPO) and related methods. SALTintroduces subspace-adaptive gradient reweighting to prevent performance plateaus when increasing rollouts, whileTAPOcorrects credit misassignment in tool-augmented agents via counterfactual advantage correction. The problem of delayed and sparse rewards is tackled byRREDCoT, which uses the model itself to redistribute credit to individual chain-of-thought segments, andTARPOprovides a unified framework for token-wise switching between discrete and latent reasoning, optimizing both with a shared advantage signal. Novel reward designs are also proliferating:OrderGradenables optimization of order-statistic objectives like CVaR,Self-supervised User Profile Generationuses a bidirectional ranking reward, andTLA-Proverleverages a formal model checker as a verifiable reward. The theoretical foundations are being strengthened byA Pre-Registered Causal Partition of RLVR, which decomposes reward effects to separate self-consistency elicitation from genuine reward design, and byMaxPO, which provides unbiased advantage estimates for max@K objectives. Finally,Agentic Monte CarloandMusaCoderdemonstrate that RL optimization can be applied to black-box agents and specialized domains like GPU kernel generation, whileReinforcement Learning Elicits Contextual Learningextends outcome-based RL to the novel domain of unseen language translation.

Latent Reasoning

The field of latent reasoning is undergoing a paradigm shift, with papers moving from proof-of-concept to establishing principled frameworks for continuous thought.NF-CoTadvances the state of the art by using normalizing flows to model continuous latent thoughts, crucially preserving autoregressive generation, KV-cache compatibility, and tractable likelihoods—addressing key practical concerns.TARPOintroduces a learned router that dynamically decides at each token whether to engage in discrete or continuous latent reasoning, effectively unifying both paradigms under a single RL-optimized policy.MPCoTextends latent reasoning to the vision-language-action domain, using a reward-guided multi-path framework to refine hypotheses in latent space without generating any reasoning tokens.Depth-Attentionproposes a novel architecture that enables cross-layer value mixing inside the attention module, performing implicit computation without adding inference-time state. However,Cosine Misleadsprovides a crucial cautionary finding: cosine alignment in latent visual reasoning is negatively correlated with accuracy, challenging common training assumptions and introducing the PRISM diagnostic toolkit.The Tell-Tale Normoffers a practical signal for controlling reasoning dynamics, demonstrating that the L2 norm of hidden states can be used for test-time scaling.LoRicontributes a low-rank distillation method for aligning teacher and student trajectories in latent subspaces, whilePretraining Recurrent Networks without Recurrenceintroduces a novel training paradigm for recurrent architectures that could serve as a foundation for future latent reasoning models.

On-Policy Distillation and Representation Learning

A refined understanding of distillation is emerging, with a strong emphasis on the on-policy paradigm and the use of privileged information.OPRD directly addresses a key weakness of prior distillation methods by aligning student and teacher hidden states on the same rollouts, eliminating sampling variance and yielding significant gains on AIME benchmarks. ViCuRintroduces a novel form of privileged information for multimodal models, replacing answer-side privilege with recoverable visual cues that guide the student during on-policy training.LoRidemonstrates that distillation for implicit reasoning can be made efficient by operating in a low-rank subspace of the teacher's trajectory.Self-Evaluation Is Already There(SEE) shows that a calibration-coupled RL phase can elicit latent self-evaluation in base LLMs, which is then transferred to the student via masked distillation.Learning Visual Spatial Planning from Modality-Gap-Aware Self-Distillation(MGSD) applies on-policy distillation from a symbolic-state teacher to bridge the modality gap for visual planning.ACE-SQLjointly optimizes schema retrieval and SQL generation using on-policy credit assignment from execution feedback, effectively distilling the search process. These works collectively demonstrate that the most effective distillation methods are those that operate on shared, on-policy trajectories and leverage recoverable or privileged signals to guide the student's learning.

Agents and Tool Use

The agent research landscape is increasingly focused on solving the credit assignment problem in complex, multi-step environments and on leveraging RL for optimizing agent behavior.TAPOprovides a direct solution for tool-augmented agents by correcting the credit misassignment that arises in GRPO when agents use external tools, showing that standard group-relative methods can be suboptimal in this setting.ACE-SQLextends this idea to the Text-to-SQL domain, jointly optimizing schema retrieval and SQL generation through on-policy credit assignment from execution feedback.Retrospective Harness Optimization(RHO) proposes a self-supervised RL-like method that allows agents to improve their own behavior by learning from self-preference over trajectory rollouts, eliminating the need for external labels.Imbuing Large Language Models with Bidirectional Logicintroduces a training framework that uses goal-conditioned bridging to repair erroneous reasoning segments in agent trajectories, directly addressing the error snowballing problem.Step-by-Step Optimization-like Reasoning (OPT*) proposes a new family of optimization-style tasks and a solver-guided online policy optimization method, providing a structured benchmark for agent reasoning over expanding search spaces.
