## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### SFT & On-Policy Distillation
A major focus today is on refining on-policy distillation (OPD) to overcome foundational limitations. **Trajectory-Refined Distillation (TRD)**directly tackles the "prefix failure" problem in on-policy reasoning, while**Stabilizing On-Policy Distillation for MLLM Reasoning**introduces global normalization to stabilize token-level KL optimization. Critically,**Escaping the KL Agreement Trap**identifies and resolves a low-KL convergence failure, and**Breaking the Tokenizer Barrier**opens OPD to cross-model-family training. These works collectively shift OPD from a heuristic technique to a more principled and robust training paradigm.

### RL for LLMs (Reinforcement Learning & RLVR)
The landscape of RL for LLMs is being redefined by innovations in reward design, training stability, and efficiency. A new theoretical framework,**RePO**, reframes RLHF as regret minimization, while**A Unifying Lens on Reward Uncertainty**provides a closed-form solution for distributional reward models. Practical advances include**sGPO**for compute-efficient training,**Sparrow**for stable long-context rollout, and**DRPO**which replaces hard clipping with a smooth divergence regularizer. The detection of emergent proxy internalization (**PRIME**) before overt reward hacking offers a crucial safety mechanism. Furthermore,**DyCo-RL**and**PRPO**introduce dynamic cross-modal and token-level advantage reshaping, respectively, for multimodal reasoning.

### Reward Modeling & Credit Assignment
Sophisticated reward and credit assignment is a dominant theme, moving beyond simple scalar rewards.**PBSD**provides Bayes-calibrated turn-level credit from sparse rewards, while**From Correctness to Utility**introduces a prefix utility model for outcome-grounded evaluation. Several works propose new reward structures:**Progress-SQL**uses an Oracle-guided diagnostic tree for progressive rewards, and**Internalizing Geometric Law**introduces saturating additive rewards for precision-critical tasks. On the model side,**PRISM**reduces false positives in Process Reward Models, and**DynaCF**mitigates shortcut learning via dynamic counterfactual sensitivity.**Beyond Rubrics**synthesizes reusable evaluation skills, and**ComplexConstraints**leverages expert rubrics for RLVR training.

### Reasoning & Inference-Time Scaling
Advances in reasoning focus on diversity, structured exploration, and scaling to non-verifiable domains.**Diverse Thinking Schemata**explicitly encourages diverse reasoning paths via DiScO, while**Reasoning Arena**uses trace tournaments to extract rich signals from verifiable rewards. For inference-time scaling,**Intrinsic Selection and Particle Resampling**extends techniques to non-verifiable domains without ground truth.**Momentum for Reasoning (ISPO)**densifies binary rewards with intrinsic policy signals, and**ConSteer-RL**introduces confidence-aware reward shaping.**When to Think Deeply**proposes an inhibitory controller for selective deep computation, and**AGCLR**advances latent reasoning with a gated persistent memory stream to overcome concept bottlenecks.

### Multimodal & Visual Reasoning
Multimodal reasoning sees targeted improvements in training stability and cross-modal coordination.**GNDPO**stabilizes on-policy distillation specifically for MLLM reasoning, while**DyCo-RL**uses Fisher-Rao geodesic distance for dynamic cross-modal coordination.**Thinking Without Images**internalizes visual manipulation into latent imagination via on-policy self-distillation. For specific tasks,**CapRL++**unifies RLVR for dense captioning, and**Temporal-Aware Reasoning Optimization (TaRO)**introduces a temporal-sensitivity reward for video grounding. On the architectural side,**Q-VGM**applies value-gradient matching for fine-tuning flow-matching VLA policies. A controlled study (**Stage-1 Controls the Entropy Regime**) reveals that initial OPD/SFT stages primarily set the entropy regime rather than determining final outcomes for VLMs.

### Agents & Tool Use
Agent training is being enhanced with hierarchical RL and cost-aware policies.**CAHL**jointly optimizes high-level planners and low-level executors via RLVR for tool-augmented LLMs.**Agentopia**introduces a long-term life simulation with a life reward for societal learning.**Translate-R1**learns a cost-aware translation tool-use policy via confidence-gated GSPO.**AliyunConsoleAgent**demonstrates a two-stage SFT+RL paradigm for real-world web agents, using a dual-channel outcome reward model.

### Specialized & Foundational Methods
Several papers address foundational issues and specialized domains.**CRANE**uses GRPO with a Cognitive Routing Reward for knowledge editing in reasoning MLLMs.**LEAF**provides tree-based credit assignment for speech-aware LLMs.**PVPO**introduces voxel-space geometric rewards for sample-efficient LEGO spatial-physics reasoning.**SAW**proposes a stage-aware dynamic weighting mechanism for multi-objective RL alignment.**Thinking-RFT**applies verifiable rewards to improve Theory of Mind reasoning.**Teacher-Free Self-Training**analyzes amplification vs. compounding in a free-verifier domain, and**A Discrepancy-Constrained MDP** stabilizes training by controlling train-inference discrepancy.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, scalable self-improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures with clear technical substance.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## 3. On-Policy Distillation for LLMs
We care about distillation methods that improve LLM capabilities by training on the model's own on-policy outputs, trajectories, preferences, corrections, or interaction data, especially when the student distribution is actively refreshed during training.
- **Strong match:** On-policy distillation, self-distillation from sampled rollouts, online teacher-student distillation, policy-improvement distillation, iterative distillation from model-generated reasoning traces, distillation from verifier/reward-selected on-policy samples, and methods that close the gap between training data distribution and the model's current behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new iterative distillation loop, sampling-and-selection strategy, online data refresh mechanism, teacher/student update schedule, or objective that measurably improves reasoning, instruction-following, alignment, tool use, or general LLM capability.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard offline knowledge distillation, static SFT on teacher-generated data, model compression only, data cleaning only, speculative decoding/distillation for speed only, or generic synthetic data generation without an on-policy or iterative policy-improvement component.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
