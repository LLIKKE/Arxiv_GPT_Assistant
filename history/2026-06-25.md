## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### Reinforcement Learning for LLMs
The dominant theme today is the maturation of RL-based training for LLMs, moving beyond simple reward design to address core algorithmic and practical challenges. Several papers tackle the critical issue of **on-policy distillation under data staleness**. AsyncOPD provides the first systematic study of this problem, demonstrating that careful estimator choices can maintain accuracy while significantly improving training throughput. Complementing this, the blockwise policy-drift gating mechanism offers a lightweight, student-only solution to control drift when reusing rollout data, directly improving math reasoning. Beyond distillation, new reward designs are emerging: the formally verified legal reward signal for self-improving AI and the Semantic Evidence Reward (SER) for video grounding both showcase how to construct more principled, verifiable, or semantically aligned reward functions. This trend is further extended by EXPO-SQL's fine-grained clause-level rewards and the adaptive importance weighting for visual reasoning in PointVG-R. Finally, work on escaping the self-confirmation trap and the persistent alignment of beneficial models highlights a growing focus on robustness and generalization in RL training, moving the field toward more reliable and broadly applicable RL-for-LLMs.

### Reasoning and Inference
A fascinating new direction is emerging that frames LLM reasoning as a form of**attractor dynamics or latent retrieval**. The paper on "Reasoning as Attractor Dynamics" proposes a Gibbs-weighted energy minimization over reasoning trajectories, treating inference as a process of converging to stable latent states. This mechanistic perspective is complemented by the EVA framework for multimodal reasoning, which introduces continuous latent slot tokens and a novel D-GSPO optimization to address policy deviation in latent reasoning. Together, these works suggest a shift away from purely token-level autoregressive reasoning toward more structured, latent-space reasoning processes that may offer efficiency and robustness benefits. The strategy-guided policy optimization (SGPO) paper further supports this trend by replacing trajectory imitation with reusable strategy distillation, effectively guiding the model's reasoning process at a higher, more abstract level.

### Agents and Tool Use
The intersection of RL and agentic systems is seeing significant innovation, particularly around**autonomous evaluation and experience learning**. The framework for computer-use agents directly tackles the reward signal bottleneck by using noisy VLM-based autonomous evaluation with a noise-corrected PPO estimator, demonstrating a practical path for scaling RL in complex, interactive environments. Simultaneously, the Execute-Distill-Verify paradigm for agentic experience learning addresses the critical issue of self-confirmation bias by introducing multi-agent distillation and consensus verification, ensuring agents learn from diverse, validated experiences rather than reinforcing their own mistakes. These works represent a crucial step toward building agents that can learn robustly from their own interactions without requiring expensive human annotation.

### Multimodal and Vision-Language Models
Multimodal reasoning is being advanced through**grounded, verifiable reward signals**. The Semantic Evidence Reward (SER) for video grounding replaces traditional geometric IoU with a referee VLM that judges semantic alignment, directly improving spatio-temporal reasoning in video MLLMs. Similarly, PointVG-R integrates geometric reasoning into MLLMs for precise pointing localization, using RL with an adaptive importance weighting reward to internalize visual chain-of-thought reasoning. Both approaches demonstrate a clear trend: moving beyond simple matching or classification rewards toward more semantically meaningful, structurally informed reward designs that can guide complex multimodal reasoning tasks.

### Robustness and Alignment
A critical focus is emerging on**improving LLM robustness against pathological inputs and distributional shifts**. The "Pigeonholing" paper identifies and addresses a specific failure mode where bad prompts cause models to collapse into erroneous states, proposing RLVR with synthetic errors to mitigate this issue. This is complemented by the work on broadly and persistently beneficial models, which uses a dataset of realistic situations to reinforce beneficial traits, demonstrating that alignment can generalize out-of-distribution in both breadth and persistence. These papers together signal a maturing understanding that alignment and robustness are not just about avoiding harmful outputs but about ensuring reliable, consistent behavior across a wide spectrum of inputs and contexts, including adversarial or unusual ones.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, scalable self-improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures with clear technical substance.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## 3. On-Policy Distillation for LLMs
We care about distillation methods that improve LLM capabilities by training on the model's own on-policy outputs, trajectories, preferences, corrections, or interaction data, especially when the student distribution is actively refreshed during training.
- **Strong match:** On-policy distillation, self-distillation from sampled rollouts, online teacher-student distillation, policy-improvement distillation, iterative distillation from model-generated reasoning traces, distillation from verifier/reward-selected on-policy samples, and methods that close the gap between training data distribution and the model's current behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new iterative distillation loop, sampling-and-selection strategy, online data refresh mechanism, teacher/student update schedule, or objective that measurably improves reasoning, instruction-following, alignment, tool use, or general LLM capability.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard offline knowledge distillation, static SFT on teacher-generated data, model compression only, data cleaning only, speculative decoding/distillation for speed only, or generic synthetic data generation without an on-policy or iterative policy-improvement component.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
