## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### Reinforcement Learning for LLMs & Alignment

Today's research demonstrates a significant push towards making Reinforcement Learning (RL) for Large Language Models (LLMs) more robust, efficient, and targeted. A key theme is the development of adaptive and structured RL methods that move beyond monolithic reward signals. **EVPO**introduces adaptive critic utilization based on explained variance, while**Policy Gradient Primal-Dual Method**provides formal convergence guarantees for safe RLHF. The community is also deeply focused on diagnosing and repairing the side-effects of alignment training, as seen in**ARES**'s framework for end-to-end system repair and the analysis of**verbal tics**and the**shared sycophancy-lying circuit**. Furthermore, novel RL objectives are being crafted for specific domains, such as**TRN-R1-Zero**for network reasoning and**DTM**for fine-tuning diffusion models, indicating RL's expanding role as a versatile post-training tool.

### Self-Evolving & Self-Improving Agents

The paradigm of agents capable of autonomous, long-horizon improvement is rapidly maturing, with research focusing on scalable learning mechanisms and stability. A clear trend is the move from static training to continuous, data-efficient self-evolution loops. Frameworks like**EasyRL**and**TEMPO**exemplify this, using progressive strategies and test-time training to refine policies. Critical to this evolution is the ability to learn from interaction, as demonstrated by agents that automatically discover compression rules (**Self-Evolving Framework for Terminal Agents**) or distill reusable skills from experience (**From Experience to Skill**). Stability and balanced exploration remain paramount, addressed by methods that aggregate latent hypotheses (**The Essence of Balance**) or use group-relative alignment (**Self-Improving Tabular Language Models**).

### Multimodal & Vision-Language-Action Models

Research in multimodal systems is increasingly integrating reinforcement learning to refine complex reasoning and generation processes. The core challenge is assigning fine-grained credit within long, structured outputs.**OTCA**addresses this for visual generation by optimizing trajectory credit, while**DT2IT-MRM**tackles bias in multimodal reward modeling to improve alignment. Several works apply RL post-training directly to embodied or action-oriented domains:**SpanVLA**enables learning from negative-recovery samples for driving, and**Multi-modal Reasoning with LLMs**uses verifiable rewards for visual semantic arithmetic. The goal is to move from passive understanding to reliable, reward-driven decision-making in multimodal contexts.

### Latent Reasoning & Process Optimization

There is a concerted effort to better understand, steer, and optimize the internal reasoning processes of models, treating them as latent variables to be aligned. Methods are becoming more sophisticated, moving from simple supervision to training-free steering or latent-space policy learning.**OLLM**models next-token options with a discrete latent variable for efficient RL, and**GRIL**teaches LLMs to pause via a multi-turn RL framework. Analysis papers provide crucial insights, showing how**answer tokens read reasoning traces**and how**reasoning structure matters for safety**. This direction seeks to make reasoning more transparent, reliable, and controllable, whether through architectural innovation (**Gradient-Based Program Synthesis**) or subspace alignment (**Discovering a Shared Logical Subspace**).

### Reward Design & Credit Assignment

Advanced reward design is recognized as fundamental to effective RL, with today's papers introducing novel structures for attribution and verification. The focus is on creating rewards that are more informative and less prone to gaming or inconsistency.**Groupwise Ranking Reward**tackles reasoning-answer inconsistency by ranking based on verifiable sub-rewards.**SAVOIR**employs Shapley values for nuanced reward attribution in social interactions, and**DR-MMSearchAgent**uses dynamic reward calibration to prevent premature collapse. These approaches move beyond binary correctness, aiming to shape the quality and integrity of the entire generation process, which is also the goal of**Distillation Traps and Guards** for calibrating teacher models.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
