## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### SFT and Data Curation
A clear trend is emerging around efficiency in supervised fine-tuning for reasoning models. Rather than relying on full-trajectory evaluation, new research demonstrates that the quality of a reasoning trace can be determined from its initial tokens alone, using perturbed model checkpoints as lightweight discriminators. This allows for highly efficient data curation pipelines that rival or exceed the performance of full-trajectory filtering, significantly reducing the computational cost of building high-quality SFT sets for reasoning.

### RL for LLMs (Reward Design & Training Stability)
The field is experiencing a profound maturation in reward design and training dynamics. A major focus is the co-evolution of rewards and policy: one paper argues that no single reward is a silver bullet and that reward design must adapt as the policy's capability grows, while another introduces a framework where agents and their evaluators recursively co-evolve. Practically, this is addressed by several new techniques: a geometric rollout curation method that detects and rectifies directionally inconsistent rollouts to stabilize online RL, and a framework using calibrated continuous rewards from score-based tasks that bypasses the need for ground-truth solutions, mitigating scale and frequency dominance. Safety alignment is also benefiting from this sophistication, with an intent-aware training scheme using GRPO to reward faithfulness to user intent rather than surface-form compliance. Furthermore, a novel RLAIF framework introduces a deterministic reward floor to combat reward hacking in industrial query generation, highlighting the critical sensitivity of RL to optimizer choice.

### On-Policy Distillation
On-policy distillation is solidifying its role as a powerful alternative or complement to pure RL. A new framework, MOPD, demonstrates that multi-teacher on-policy distillation can outperform GRPO RL on instruction-following tasks for an 8B model, providing a highly transparent and ablated post-training pipeline. This is complemented by a self-evolving framework for multimodal models that uses self-consistency rewards and internal role decomposition to drive unified understanding and generation. For agentic settings, a dedicated on-policy skill distillation method (OPID) extracts hierarchical skill supervision from completed trajectories to provide dense, token-level advantages, effectively bootstrapping RL for language agents. A weakness-driven coevolution framework also leverages RLVR with graph-based structural generation to create a closed-loop data-model coevolution system for optimization modeling.

### Multimodal Understanding & Generation
A critical limitation of multimodal LLMs—"visual laziness"—is being directly addressed through RL-based post-training. A new framework introduces a geometric constraint that maximizes mutual information between the visual input and the model's response, using a novel counterfactual blind state penalty to explicitly penalize the model for ignoring visual evidence. This represents a significant step towards ensuring that MLLMs genuinely ground their reasoning in visual data rather than relying on language priors. Separately, a unified framework for multimodal understanding and generation leverages self-evolution and self-consistency rewards, demonstrating a path toward models that can both perceive and create with equal fidelity.

### Compression & Efficiency
Research into compression reveals a critical fragility in modern reasoning architectures. Aggressive compression, such as INT4 quantization, can catastrophically destroy the global reasoning capabilities of recursive (latent) reasoners. The key insight is that this degradation can be recovered through per-channel calibrated quantization, suggesting that compression strategies must be designed with an explicit awareness of the model's recursive reasoning topology, not just its parameter count.

### Mechanistic Interpretability & Model Steering
A breakthrough in mechanistic interpretability now allows for precise localization and steering of RL-induced behaviors. By using Dedicated Feature Crosscoders, researchers have isolated a single crosscoder feature responsible for tool-use behavior induced by RL training. This enables retraining-free behavioral control, allowing practitioners to modulate or remove learned capabilities without costly re-training, and even observe capability spillover to related tasks.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, scalable self-improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures with clear technical substance.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## 3. On-Policy Distillation for LLMs
We care about distillation methods that improve LLM capabilities by training on the model's own on-policy outputs, trajectories, preferences, corrections, or interaction data, especially when the student distribution is actively refreshed during training.
- **Strong match:** On-policy distillation, self-distillation from sampled rollouts, online teacher-student distillation, policy-improvement distillation, iterative distillation from model-generated reasoning traces, distillation from verifier/reward-selected on-policy samples, and methods that close the gap between training data distribution and the model's current behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new iterative distillation loop, sampling-and-selection strategy, online data refresh mechanism, teacher/student update schedule, or objective that measurably improves reasoning, instruction-following, alignment, tool use, or general LLM capability.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard offline knowledge distillation, static SFT on teacher-generated data, model compression only, data cleaning only, speculative decoding/distillation for speed only, or generic synthetic data generation without an on-policy or iterative policy-improvement component.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
