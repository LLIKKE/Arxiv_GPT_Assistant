## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs
A significant cluster of papers advances reinforcement learning for large language models, focusing on improving training efficiency, credit assignment, and reward design. **EchoRL**and**DRIFT**tackle the inefficiency of full rollouts, with EchoRL reusing advantage-degenerated trajectories via entropy-based clipping, while DRIFT decouples rollouts from optimization using importance-weighted SFT to match multi-turn performance with SFT-level efficiency.**VeriGate**and**Graph-GRPO**address the sparse reward problem in GRPO by introducing step-level credit assignment through verifier-gated process supervision and dependency-aware propagation over graph structures, respectively.**DARTS**accelerates training with distribution-aware active trajectory shaping, and**S2L-PO**proposes using smaller models as natural explorers to enhance rollout diversity. On the reward model side,**SAVE**introduces on-policy value-anchored feedback for self-supervised reward model improvement, while**Configurable Reward Model**enables balanced safety alignment. A critical theoretical contribution from**Annealed Softmax Greedy**provides a structural analogy for GRPO-style updates in many-armed bandits, explaining their empirical behavior. Notably,**Representation Collapse**systematically studies plasticity loss in sequential RL-based post-training, proposing interventions to preserve model capacity.

### Self-Evolving Agents & Open-Ended Learning
Several papers push the frontier of autonomous agent improvement through co-evolution and self-play.**SCOPE**introduces a data-free framework where Challenger and Solver policies co-evolve for open-ended tasks, directly modeling the self-improving agent paradigm.**Harness Updating Is Not Harness Benefit**provides a sobering analysis, revealing that self-evolving capabilities are surprisingly flat and non-monotonic across model tiers, cautioning against conflating the ability to update with actual performance gains.**AdaCoM**and**ElasticMem**explore learned context management, training external LLMs via RL to compress and adaptively allocate latent memory budgets for frozen agents.**ExpGraph**proposes a model-agnostic experience learning framework with self-evolving graph memory and an RL-trained retrieval copilot.**Skill Reuse as Compression**grounds agentic RL in the Minimum Description Length principle, extracting reusable skill dictionaries from trajectories.**SCALE**and**ReGuLaR**further demonstrate self-improvement through adversarial graph exploration and relation-grounded latent reasoning, respectively.

### Multimodal RL & Grounding
Reinforcement learning is being increasingly applied to multimodal models, with a focus on grounding visual reasoning and mitigating hallucinations.**iVGR**internalizes visual grounding into textual reasoning via a consistency reward in an RL framework.**EASE**augments multimodal RLVR with visual-evidence process supervision by guiding attention maps.**IC-VCO**proposes an RL-based visual preference optimization method with contrastive sample editing to directly mitigate hallucinations.**FOCUS**uses GRPO to minimize localization error in VLMs.**DriveMA**extends this to driving, using verifiable meta-actions with turn-level credit assignment for aligning high-level decisions with low-level planning.**TaskMem**learns a memorization policy for multimodal agents via RL, enabling self-improvement from environment interaction.

### Reasoning Efficiency & Latent Reasoning
Improving the efficiency of chain-of-thought reasoning remains a central theme, with novel approaches emerging for adaptive budget allocation and compressed representations.**SLAT**and**AdaptR1**use RL to learn segment-level adaptive trimming and quality-gated reasoning budgets, respectively, reducing redundancy without sacrificing accuracy.**Chain-of-Thought and Compressed Looped Transformers**provides a theoretical separation between the memory budgets of explicit CoT and compressed latent loops, clarifying the limitations of latent reasoning.**Efficient Diffusion LLMs**proposes temporal-spatial parallel decoding for latency reduction in diffusion-based LLMs.**LinTree**improves reasoning by explicitly representing the tree structure of search histories, outperforming implicit representations.

### Distillation & Training Recipes
Several papers refine on-policy distillation strategies for reasoning models.**Are Full Rollouts Necessary for On-Policy Distillation?**introduces horizon-control strategies (Progressive/Truncated OPD) to improve efficiency for long-horizon tasks.**Trust-Region Behavior Blending**proposes a KL-constrained warmup to improve student rollout quality.**Your Teacher Can't Help You Here**identifies and mitigates supervision fidelity decay in on-policy distillation using a Lookahead Group Reward.**Distilling LLM Feedback**for theorem proving uses token-level supervision to improve policy entropy, outperforming GRPO.

### Specialized Applications & Safety
RL is being applied to specialized domains with unique reward structures.**Graph-GRPO**and**Planner-Centric RL**address e-commerce search and deep research with structure-aware DAG rewards.**Automating Formal Verification**uses RLVR with GRPO for theorem proving, tackling specification hacking.**PA-DR**combines task rewards with a learned privacy classifier for deep research agents.**DareU**proposes an RL-based unlearning framework using data attribution rewards.**Reinforcement Learning Amplifies Emergent Misalignment**provides a cautionary result, showing that harmless rewards can lead to misalignment when amplified by RL in small models.**Inverse Reinforcement Learning without an Optimal Demonstrator** offers a theoretical framework for learning from suboptimal demonstrations, applicable to LLM fine-tuning.

To address these issues, we introduce \textsc{LongTraceRL}. For data construction, we generate multi-hop questions via knowledge graph random walks and leverage search agent trajectories to build \emph{tiered distractors}: documents the agent read but did not cite (high confusability) and documents that appeared in search results but were never opened (low confusability), producing training contexts that are far more challenging than those built by random sampling or one-shot search. # Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
