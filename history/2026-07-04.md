## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs
The field is experiencing a surge in novel reward design and training frameworks, moving beyond simple scalar rewards to more nuanced signals. Several papers introduce sophisticated reward structures: Maven proposes action-level rewards grounded in an editable evidence memory for long-context reasoning, while LASER uses dual rewards for visual attention regulation in LVLMs. The Distributionally Robust Listwise Preference Optimization paper advances the theoretical foundation of reward modeling with a worst-case correction, and the Multi-Role Rubric Generation framework improves RLVR by automating rubric creation. Stability and scaling are also key themes, with FADE introducing a self-adapting advantage function to dynamically balance gradient weights, and the lie detector oversight paper showing promising scaling trends for removing human labelers in preference learning. These works collectively indicate a maturation of RL for LLMs, focusing on more granular, stable, and automated reward processes to achieve robust alignment and reasoning.

### On-Policy Self-Distillation
A major critical analysis and refinement of on-policy self-distillation (SDPO) is underway, revealing both its fundamental limitations and novel solutions. The Denser ≠ Better paper provides a crucial analysis of SDPO's collapse risks in continual post-training, setting the stage for three targeted fixes. Purified OPSD diagnoses and isolates a failure mode related to privileged information leakage by distilling only the inference-transferable correction signal. DemoPSD tackles the same issue through disagreement-modulated blending of teacher and student distributions to preserve exploration. Neuron-OPSD introduces a data-centric approach, using internal neuron activations for smarter data selection and teacher construction. This concentrated effort suggests the community is systematically addressing the core weaknesses of on-policy distillation, moving towards more robust and self-sustaining training loops.

### RL for Reasoning & Agents
Reinforcement learning is being directly applied to enhance structured reasoning and agentic capabilities, often with verifiable rewards. DecompRL shifts the paradigm by learning to decompose problems into modular code, effectively moving the computational bottleneck from GPU sampling to CPU evaluation and solving problems previously intractable for standard RL. In the agentic domain, the RLVR proof-of-concept on Atlassian workflows demonstrates that GRPO training on synthetic API environments yields strong reward gains for tool-use agents. The FaithMed paper applies step-level process reward RL with clinician-designed rubrics to enforce faithful evidence-based medical reasoning. These works show a clear trend of using RL to instill structured thinking and verifiable action sequences, moving LLMs from next-token prediction to goal-directed problem-solving.

### Multimodal & Vision-Language Models
RL is becoming a core training paradigm for improving reasoning in vision-language models (LVLMs), specifically targeting visual grounding and self-reflection. The LASER framework introduces a post-training RL approach with rewards for visual attention preservation and sink suppression, directly improving reasoning by ensuring the model attends to the correct visual regions. The VRRL paper takes this further by using masked trajectory prefixes and buffered roll-ins to elicit visually grounded self-reflection, showing strong gains in out-of-distribution visual reasoning. These works highlight a shift from purely language-based RL to multimodal RL that explicitly governs the interaction between visual and textual modalities, addressing a key weakness of current LVLMs.

### Alignment & Confidence
New directions in alignment are emerging that focus on multi-objective optimization and confidence calibration. The MI-EPO framework uses mutual information to unify exploration and preference optimization for multi-objective alignment, providing a principled information-theoretic approach. Separately, the C3RL algorithm jointly optimizes for correctness and confidence calibration, enabling adaptive test-time scaling (CAS) that dynamically allocates compute based on a model's stated confidence. These papers point to a future where alignment is not just about matching human preferences but also about managing the model's own epistemic state and balancing multiple, potentially competing, objectives in a principled manner.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, scalable self-improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures with clear technical substance.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## 3. On-Policy Distillation for LLMs
We care about distillation methods that improve LLM capabilities by training on the model's own on-policy outputs, trajectories, preferences, corrections, or interaction data, especially when the student distribution is actively refreshed during training.
- **Strong match:** On-policy distillation, self-distillation from sampled rollouts, online teacher-student distillation, policy-improvement distillation, iterative distillation from model-generated reasoning traces, distillation from verifier/reward-selected on-policy samples, and methods that close the gap between training data distribution and the model's current behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new iterative distillation loop, sampling-and-selection strategy, online data refresh mechanism, teacher/student update schedule, or objective that measurably improves reasoning, instruction-following, alignment, tool use, or general LLM capability.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard offline knowledge distillation, static SFT on teacher-generated data, model compression only, data cleaning only, speculative decoding/distillation for speed only, or generic synthetic data generation without an on-policy or iterative policy-improvement component.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
