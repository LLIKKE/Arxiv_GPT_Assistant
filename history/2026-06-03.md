## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### Latent Reasoning & Interpretability
A significant cluster of papers pushes beyond the black box of chain-of-thought, focusing on latent reasoning—manipulating and understanding internal model computations. Works propose training-free decode-time interventions guided by mechanistic interpretability and introduce novel architectures like Geometric Latent Reasoning (GLR) that learn continuous trajectories in embedding space for efficiency. However, the field is also engaging in critical self-reflection; mechanistic diagnostics reveal that performance gains in latent visual reasoning may stem from format markers rather than true latent "slots," challenging core assumptions. This tension between advancing new latent architectures and rigorously validating their underlying mechanisms defines the current frontier. Parallel frameworks extend this paradigm to multimodal domains, including vision-language-action and speech recognition, suggesting a broad trend toward internalizing reasoning processes.

### RL for LLMs: Theory, Optimization, and Alignment
This area is experiencing a renaissance of theoretical and methodological rigor. A key theoretical contribution provides a causal information-theoretic bound explaining why outcome-based RL leads to reasoning shortcuts, formally justifying process reward models as a topological filter. This is complemented by a flurry of practical innovations addressing critical bottlenecks: credit assignment (via adapter residuals for LoRA, step-level advantages), entropy collapse (via on-policy self-distillation as a "policy reheater"), and sample inefficiency (via prioritized group replay). New optimization recipes like Hybrid Median-length Policy Optimization (HMPO) directly tackle CoT compression, while frameworks for Nash preference optimization and semantic-calibrated self-play (S-SPPO) refine alignment. The trend is toward more granular, theoretically grounded, and efficient RL training recipes that move beyond simple outcome rewards.

### Self-Improving & Self-Evolving Agents
The agent landscape is shifting from static tool use to dynamic, closed-loop self-improvement. A core theme is the co-evolution of agent components—world models and policies (COMAP), skills and tools (SkillSmith), or skills and memory (MemPro). These frameworks leverage self-distillation, trajectory-level credit assignment, and failure-mode-guided revision to create a continuous improvement loop without external supervision. Novel approaches like ExpWeaver use latent retrieval-augmented generation from hidden states, while others focus on internalizing skills through RL (SIRI) or distilling in-the-wild guides (MMG2Skill). This represents a maturation from simple RL fine-tuning to sophisticated, multi-component evolutionary systems that learn from their own experience.

### Visual & Multimodal Reasoning with RL
Reinforcement learning is being aggressively applied to visual and multimodal domains, moving beyond static benchmarks. The introduction of TRON provides a controllable, verifiable online environment for visual reasoning RL, enabling unbounded curriculum learning. This is paired with novel architectures like DeepLatent for parallel latent visual reasoning and frameworks for active 3D viewpoint control using GRPO. A strong focus is on mitigating biases specific to multimodal models, such as spatial lexical bias via DPO and perceptual judgment bias in MLLM-as-a-Judge via GRPO-based reward modeling. The trend is toward creating verifiable, interactive training loops that ground reasoning in perceptual data, with applications ranging from aerial navigation to radiology report generation.

### Training Efficiency & Data Synthesis
Significant engineering and algorithmic progress is being made on the practicalities of large-scale RL training. Innovations address the straggler problem in synchronous on-policy RL with dynamic group sizing, and propose logit-free on-policy distillation (OmniOPD) for faster, more scalable training. On the data side, the "data flywheel" concept is becoming operationalized, as seen in HomeFlow's verifiable simulation for smart home agents and the use of agentic deep research to construct fine-grained rubrics for GRPO (DR-rubric). This shift from static datasets to dynamically generated, verifiable training data is a crucial enabler for scaling RL-based reasoning and agent training.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
