💡 今日研究速览 (Daily Summary)

RL for LLMs

A significant wave of research is solidifying Reinforcement Learning as the dominant paradigm for post-training LLMs, moving beyond simple supervised fine-tuning. Several works provide deep theoretical and practical foundations for this shift. Predictable GRPOoffers a closed-form model of GRPO training dynamics, enabling precise predictions of reward trajectories and stability thresholds, a crucial step towards making RL training more predictable and controllable. Concurrently,On the Convergence of Self-Improving Online LLM Alignment(SAIL-RevKL) tackles convergence guarantees by proposing a regularized objective that satisfies the Polyak-Łojasiewicz condition. The theme of improving credit assignment is also strong:TRIAGEintroduces role-typed process rewards to correct blind spots in outcome-only GRPO for agentic tasks, whileECHOproposes selective turn-memory for traceable on-policy credit assignment in long-horizon agentic scenarios. Practical innovations includeGradient Smoothing, a novel depth-wise gradient augmentation method shown to improve optimization and generalization in RL post-training for reasoning, andAddressing Over-Refusal in LLMs with Competing Rewards(SEAR), which uses adversarial RL with competing process rewards to explore harmful reasoning and mitigate over-refusal. Finally, frameworks likeGR2andMetaFlowdemonstrate the power of combining reasoning distillation or search with RL and verifiable rewards for industrial recommendation and workflow generation, respectively.

On-Policy Distillation & Alignment

A clear theoretical and practical consensus is emerging around the superiority of on-policy distillation over standard behavior cloning (SFT), especially when dealing with imperfect or noisy supervision.Behavior Cloning is Not All You Needprovides a rigorous theoretical foundation, proving the optimality of on-policy distillation (OPD) with noisy expert feedback and proposing a variant with polynomial horizon dependence. This principle is applied across diverse domains:GR2employs OPD as a scalable alternative to SFT for industrial recommendation, andForaintroduces a function-space protection method for capability-preserving fine-tuning that uses an on-policy distillation-like approach. The alignment literature also reflects this trend, withIntrospective Couplingshowing that fixed counterfactual explanation training (a form of self-supervision) can yield faithful introspection that tracks behavioral changes, offering a novel, scalable post-training signal.Reinforcement Learning with Metacognitive Feedback(RLMF) proposes a new RL training paradigm to elicit faithful uncertainty expression, further expanding the toolbox beyond standard RLHF.

Multimodal Reasoning & Alignment

Multimodal research is deeply integrating RL and preference optimization to tackle core challenges like hallucination and robust reasoning.Consistency as Inductive Bias(ConsistRoll) introduces a novel RLVR method that injects cross-view consistency via group-sampling in GRPO, directly improving multimodal reasoning without extra generation overhead. For hallucination mitigation, two distinct RL-based approaches stand out:Clearer Sight, Fewer Lies(OPPO) proposes a preference optimization objective that directly aligns generation with visual evidence strength using ordered evidence margins, whileVision-driven Preference Synthesisconstructs policy-aligned and visually grounded preference pairs for RLHF-style alignment. In the robotics domain,Z-1demonstrates that applying GRPO to flow-based Vision-Language-Action models yields substantial improvements over SFT. For agentic tasks,ReGRPOintroduces reflection-augmented policy optimization for tool-using VLM agents, andPersonalizing MLLMsuses a reinforced multimodal reference game with verifiable contrastive rewards to train discriminative concept descriptions.

Reasoning & Latent Computation

The frontier of efficient reasoning is being pushed by methods that operate in latent space to reduce computational overhead without sacrificing performance.Bridging the Gap Between Latent and Explicit Reasoningachieves a major milestone by being the first latent-CoT method to match the performance of explicit chain-of-thought at the 3B scale, using looped Transformers with parallel supervision on latent tokens to cut latency significantly. This is complemented by architectural innovations likeWhich Tokens Matter?, which introduces a principled token-selection method (RSI) for RLVR that reconciles conflicting prior findings and improves reasoning accuracy.Evo-PIproposes a co-evolutionary framework where language-based principles are iteratively refined to supervise LLM reasoning, acting as a dynamic RL-like reward signal. Furthermore,Geometry-Preserving Orthonormal Initialization for Low-Rank Adaptation in RLVRprovides a theoretically grounded initialization for LoRA that improves stability and performance in RL-based reasoning training.

Agentic RL & Tool Use

Agentic RL is rapidly maturing, with a focus on solving credit assignment over long horizons and enabling effective tool use.TRIAGEandECHOboth address the fundamental problem of sparse rewards in agentic tasks, with TRIAGE using semantic role-conditioned process rewards and ECHO using selective turn-memory for traceable credit assignment.ReGRPOintroduces reflection tokens and structured failure data to improve tool-using VLM agents.Xiaomi-GUI-0presents a practical, real-device closed-loop training pipeline for GUI agents using step-level and agentic RL. For multilingual settings,Think in English, Answer in Koreanproposes RL with verifiable rewards and language-consistency rewards to efficiently adapt tool-use agents. Finally,Learning to Select, Not Relearn introduces a hard-routed mixture of reasoning LoRAs, where the router is trained via on-policy distillation of reasoning traces, demonstrating a sophisticated interplay between modular architecture and RL-based training.
