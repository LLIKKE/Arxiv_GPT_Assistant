## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs
A major wave of papers today focuses on stabilizing and improving reinforcement learning for LLMs, particularly addressing credit assignment and exploration challenges. Several works tackle the instability of multi-step tool-use RL, identifying catastrophic collapse and proposing supervisory signals like interleaved SFT to stabilize training. Novel advantage estimators are a key theme: one paper derives an implicit "progress advantage" from standard post-training to enable annotation-free step-level scoring for agents, while another introduces a bisimulation-guided estimator to fix state-action credit mismatch in group-based RL. Token-level credit assignment is further refined by a method using a Monte Carlo Pass@k critic for single-rollout settings, and by a semantic consistency approach that recovers step-level credit from successful siblings within rollout groups. Exploration is also addressed, with a framework combining novelty rewards and entropy-guided prefix regeneration to improve RLVR. Finally, a transfer-aware curriculum using gradient-geometry alignment prioritizes domains that most benefit multi-domain reasoning, while another work replaces external reward models with intrinsic perplexity-based rewards for open-ended reasoning.

### On-Policy Distillation and Self-Improvement
On-policy distillation and self-improvement are being both advanced and critically examined. Several frameworks propose novel on-policy methods: one uses advantage-guided self-distillation for robust online adaptation of vision-language-action models, another employs contrastive evidence gating for answer-label-free distillation in visual reasoning, and a third introduces self-family distillation with rejection-sampling cold-start for GUI grounding. Direct policy-based safety alignment is also achieved through on-policy self-distillation from synthesized instructions. However, a crucial theoretical and empirical analysis reveals a key limitation: on-policy self-distillation with sampled demonstrations reduces output diversity. This tension is explored in a competition-guided framework where a self-improving teacher and case-wise reward design optimize chain-of-thought reasoning, and in a latent-variable fine-tuning method that interleaves grammar symbols for structural reasoning in code.

### Agents and Reasoning
Agentic systems and reasoning are seeing significant advances in trustworthiness, exploration, and structural understanding. For long-term memory in agents, a framework uses a Memory Transition Verifier and preference-guided RL to directly optimize memory update behaviors, improving trustworthiness. In reasoning, the concept of "cliff tokens" is introduced as precise failure triggers in mathematical reasoning, with a single-token preference optimization method that improves accuracy. A critical finding challenges the assumption that thinking tokens improve safety, showing that refusal/compliance is locked early and deliberation is superficial, with direct implications for latent reasoning. New frameworks propose reasoning-to-model-and-solve for optimization problems using hierarchical rewards, and a "Weave of Formal Thought" method that interleaves grammar symbols for latent structural reasoning in code.

### Evaluation and Generalization
A new evaluation framework, the Generalization Spectrum, provides a chromatographic approach to assessing learning algorithms, revealing that RL converts memorization into near-transfer more efficiently than SFT. This has direct implications for understanding the benefits of on-policy distillation and RL-based training paradigms.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, scalable self-improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures with clear technical substance.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## 3. On-Policy Distillation for LLMs
We care about distillation methods that improve LLM capabilities by training on the model's own on-policy outputs, trajectories, preferences, corrections, or interaction data, especially when the student distribution is actively refreshed during training.
- **Strong match:** On-policy distillation, self-distillation from sampled rollouts, online teacher-student distillation, policy-improvement distillation, iterative distillation from model-generated reasoning traces, distillation from verifier/reward-selected on-policy samples, and methods that close the gap between training data distribution and the model's current behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new iterative distillation loop, sampling-and-selection strategy, online data refresh mechanism, teacher/student update schedule, or objective that measurably improves reasoning, instruction-following, alignment, tool use, or general LLM capability.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard offline knowledge distillation, static SFT on teacher-generated data, model compression only, data cleaning only, speculative decoding/distillation for speed only, or generic synthetic data generation without an on-policy or iterative policy-improvement component.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
