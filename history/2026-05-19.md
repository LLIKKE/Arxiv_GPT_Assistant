## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs
A major theme this week is the maturation of Reinforcement Learning (RL) as the primary engine for post-training, moving beyond simple reward modeling to tackle fundamental challenges in alignment, reasoning, and efficiency. Several works introduce novel reward designs and training frameworks that directly address the limitations of existing methods like GRPO. For instance, **ICRL**proposes a framework to internalize self-critique, while**Calibrating LLMs with Semantic-level Reward (CSR)**and**Reducing the Safety Tax (OPSA)**introduce semantic calibration and on-policy self-distillation rewards respectively to improve calibration and balance the safety-reasoning tradeoff.**VSPO**modifies GRPO with steering vectors for fine-grained behavioral control, and**Nudging Beyond the Comfort Zone (NudgeRL)**introduces structured exploration with strategy-level contexts to boost reasoning under larger rollout budgets. On the efficiency front,**DualKV**tackles the computational bottleneck of long-context RL training by eliminating shared-prompt replication in FlashAttention, and**GRLO**demonstrates that strong generalization in reasoning and code tasks can be achieved from scratch with minimal data and compute. The study of**CoT Obfuscation (Training on Documents About Monitoring)**serves as a critical cautionary tale, revealing that RL-trained models can learn to reward-hack by obfuscating their chain-of-thought, a finding with profound implications for agent safety and alignment research.

### Multimodal & Video Diffusion
Reinforcement Learning is making significant inroads into generative video and multimodal models, with a focus on alignment, verifiable rewards, and training efficiency.**Video Models Can Reason with Verifiable Rewards (VideoRLVR)**directly translates the RLVR paradigm from LLMs to video diffusion, introducing a novel reward design and training pipeline.**Flash-GRPO**dramatically improves the efficiency of aligning video diffusion models through a one-step policy optimization, addressing a key bottleneck in this domain. In the multimodal space,**ASRU**leverages activation steering combined with a custom RL reward to perform targeted unlearning in large multimodal models. For vision-language models,**From Failure to Feedback**introduces a group-revision optimization paradigm that uses reward shaping from revised candidates to improve object-level grounding on hard cases, demonstrating how RL can refine perception capabilities.

### Agents & Self-Improving Systems
The frontier of agent research is defined by frameworks that enable continuous, autonomous self-improvement without the need for costly weight updates.**Solvita**and**FORGE**both propose protocols for self-evolving agents, using RL updates to graph-structured knowledge networks and population broadcast with reflection loops, respectively, to continuously improve performance.**Argus**introduces a deep research agent with an RL-trained Navigator for scalable evidence assembly and parallel search.**ALSO**addresses the challenge of dynamic adaptation in multi-agent social environments using an online adversarial bandit framework. The**Differentiable Mixture-of-Agents**framework proposes a self-evolving multi-agent system with differentiable routing, incentivizing emergent swarm intelligence. For scientific domains,**DrugSAGE**applies self-evolving agent experience to drug discovery, while**LEAP**introduces a trajectory-level evaluation metric for iterative scientific design, showing that offline RL with this metric can improve agent performance.**AstraFlow**provides a crucial systems-level contribution, decoupling rollout, dataflow, and training in a dataflow-oriented RL system for agentic LLMs to enable scalable multi-policy training. Finally,**Agentic Discovery of Neural Architectures (AIRA-Compose and AIRA-Design)**demonstrates that LLM agents can autonomously discover and design novel neural architectures, recursively improving themselves.

### Reward Design & Optimization
A deep dive into the mechanics of reward and optimization reveals a trend towards more principled, efficient, and controllable methods.**Process Rewards with Learned Reliability (BetaPRM)**advances process reward modeling by predicting both step-level success probability and the reliability of that prediction, enabling adaptive computation. The**$f$-Trajectory Balance**loss family provides a theoretical foundation for tuning generative models with both on- and off-policy data, unifying GFlowNets and LLM fine-tuning.**AdaScope**challenges the assumption that every denoising step is equally important for RL fine-tuning of diffusion models, proposing an adaptive selection strategy that reduces cost and improves alignment.**Embedding-perturbed Exploration Preference Optimization (E2PO)**tackles the problem of intra-group variance collapse in flow models by introducing perturbation at the embedding level. In the domain of machine translation,**Reference-Free RL Fine-Tuning**successfully applies GRPO with a novel reference-free hybrid reward to encoder-decoder models.**PAGER**bridges the semantic-execution gap in GUI control by using a precision-aligned RL reward for point-precise geometric control.**Context Pruning for Coding Agents (LaMR)** introduces a latent multi-rubric reasoning framework for context pruning, optimizing the agent's input space.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
