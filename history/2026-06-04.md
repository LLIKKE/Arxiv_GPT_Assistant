## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### SFT & Alignment
A significant cluster of work addresses fundamental limitations in current RL-based alignment pipelines. Research reveals that consistency training can paradoxically entrench sycophancy, highlighting a critical failure mode in naive alignment approaches. To combat reward hacking, novel methods like HARVE propose training-free editing of reward heads, while mechanistic taxonomies systematically categorize collapse and evaluator gaming in RLHF. On the synthesis side, frameworks like QUBRIC enable RL beyond verifiable rewards by co-designing queries and rubrics, and synthetic augmentation methods are proposed to scale RLVR tasks, reducing reliance on expensive human curation.

### RL for LLMs (Reasoning & Post-Training)
This category sees the most intense activity, with a clear focus on refining reward signals and training dynamics. Several works introduce fine-grained process rewards: ARBOR uses a reusable rubric buffer for search agents, ThoughtFold penalizes redundant reasoning steps via introspective preference learning, and Hidden-Align aligns hidden states of correct rollouts. New optimization paradigms emerge, including ASymPO for asynchronous post-training without behavior probabilities, Physics-Guided Policy Optimization with self-distillation, and GRZO, a zeroth-order optimizer that reduces gradient variance. Efficient hyperparameter optimization and federated variants like FGRPO (for non-IID data) address scalability, while methods like FiRe-OPD rethink optimization granularity by jointly filtering and reweighting trajectories. A notable trend is the exploration of diversity, with HDPO incentivizing diverse solution exploration and a principled framework using reward uncertainty to induce varied behaviors.

### Latent Reasoning & Efficiency
The field is actively redefining how reasoning is structured, moving beyond monolithic Chain-of-Thought. Hierarchical and adaptive latent reasoning models dominate, with works like ALAR switching between latent and explicit CoT based on task difficulty, and HybridThinker compressing CoT into memory tokens. Subgoal persistence (in "When to Re-Plan") and metric-space grounding (MeRa) address the stability-adaptivity tradeoff. For multimodal reasoning, SpecFlow uses a spectral-progressive approach for bounded memory, and Imaginative Perception Tokens externalize intermediate perceptual representations. Efficiency extends to inference, with RL-based controllers for dynamic exit layers (LEDE) and adaptive sampling at test time, alongside value-aware stochastic KV cache eviction for long reasoning chains.

### Agents (Self-Improving & Multi-Agent)
A major paradigm shift is the emergence of self-evolving agents that learn and consolidate skills over time. Frameworks like SkillPyramid and EvoDS enable hierarchical skill consolidation and context management via agentic RL. The "Sleep" paradigm for LLMs uses RL-based imitation and dreaming for continual self-improvement. Multi-agent systems are evolving into decentralized economies (Economy of Minds) with socialized evolution (SAGE) and federated skill learning (FederatedSkill). Tool use is a key focus, with PROVE using programmatic rewards in live environments and TAO-RL coupling tool-aware filtering with entropy-guided exploration. Practical applications include safety-critical autonomous driving (EvoDrive) and patient trajectory modeling (Traj-Evolve). A pedagogical insight from training terminal agents reveals that lower-scoring agents can paradoxically produce better teaching data due to environment-grounded supervision.

### Multimodal & Visual Reasoning
Visual reasoning is being tackled through specialized RL frameworks and latent reasoning mechanisms. VEPO integrates visual sensitivity with token entropy for credit assignment, while DCRL uses verifiable rewards for spatial reasoning. P^2-DPO grounds hallucination in perceptual processing via calibration. The trend is toward grounding abstract reasoning in the underlying visual or spatial metric space, as seen with MeRa and Imaginative Perception Tokens. Experience-driven self-evolution also extends to text-to-image generation (MemoGen), where past experiences improve future outputs.

### Compression & Efficiency
Efficiency is being addressed at multiple levels. For reasoning, HybridThinker and SpecFlow compress intermediate thoughts into compact representations. At the inference level, RL-based controllers dynamically manage compute (e.g., speculative decoding exits, adaptive sampling). KV cache management sees a novel training-free method that protects large-magnitude value states. On the training side, GRZO and efficient hyperparameter optimization reduce the computational burden of RL post-training.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
