### RL for LLMs and Reasoning
Reinforcement learning continues to dominate the LLM post-training landscape, with several papers pushing the boundaries of both algorithmic design and reward modeling. A clear trend is the move beyond simple outcome-based rewards toward more granular and theoretically grounded credit assignment. **GRPO-VPS**introduces verifiable process supervision, directly addressing the overthinking and credit assignment problems in reasoning by using process-level verification within the GRPO framework. Complementing this,**Process Supervision via Verbal Critique**establishes critique granularity as a new axis of inference-time scaling, using structured natural-language feedback from a stronger model to guide iterative refinement without additional training. On the algorithmic frontier,**Near-Future Policy Optimization (NPO)**proposes a mixed-policy scheme that leverages a model's own later checkpoints as auxiliary trajectories, improving both convergence and performance ceilings.**polyGRPO**treats language itself as a latent variable for reasoning optimization, directly aligning RL optimization with latent reasoning criteria. Finally,**MGDA-Decoupled**provides a geometry-aware multi-objective optimization approach for DPO-based alignment, explicitly accounting for each objective's convergence dynamics, while**Scaling Self-Play with Self-Guidance**introduces a novel self-play paradigm with a "Guide" role to prevent reward hacking in formal theorem proving.

### Agents and Self-Improvement
The field of LLM agents is rapidly maturing, with a strong focus on creating scalable, self-improving systems that can operate in complex, open-ended environments. A key theme is the use of dual or multi-level learning loops to automate both agent behavior and the underlying infrastructure.**AgenticQwen**proposes a dual data flywheel combining reasoning RL and agentic RL, enabling small models to self-improve via synthetic data generation and difficulty escalation. Similarly,**AEL (Agent Evolving Learning)**introduces a two-timescale framework with Thompson Sampling for memory retrieval and LLM-driven reflection, creating a truly self-evolving agent.**Co-Evolving LLM Decision and Skill Bank Agents (COSPLAY)**takes this further by having a decision agent and a skill bank agent mutually improve through unlabeled rollouts. On the infrastructure side,**The Last Harness You'll Ever Build**proposes a two-level meta-learning framework that automates harness engineering, allowing agents to self-improve via evolution loops.**DR-Venus**demonstrates that frontier-level deep research agents can be trained with only 10K open data points using a combination of agentic SFT and turn-level RL, making such capabilities more accessible.

### Latent Reasoning and Efficiency
A growing body of work is focused on making LLM reasoning more efficient by compressing the reasoning process into reusable components or by focusing computational resources on the most relevant information.**Thinking with Reasoning Skills**directly addresses token efficiency by distilling deliberation into reusable "reasoning skills," achieving higher accuracy with fewer tokens.**Foveated Reasoning**applies RL to learn a foveation policy for vision-language models, allowing the model to focus on salient image regions and perform efficient latent reasoning.**Language as a Latent Variable for Reasoning Optimization (polyGRPO)**also falls into this category, treating language as a latent variable to optimize the reasoning process itself. Finally,**Differentiable Conformal Training**offers a novel approach to improving factuality in reasoning by learning a scorer that maximizes claim retention while maintaining formal reliability guarantees, bridging the gap between efficiency and trustworthiness.

### Multimodal and Domain-Specific RL
Reinforcement learning is being increasingly applied to multimodal models and specialized domains, often requiring novel reward designs that capture the unique characteristics of these tasks.**Rethinking Reinforcement Fine-Tuning in LVLM**provides a theoretical foundation for RLVR in large vision-language models, proving convergence of GRPO under composite rewards and establishing a reward decomposition theorem.**Reinforcing 3D Understanding in Point-VLMs**introduces geometric reward credit assignment to improve 3D comprehension, demonstrating how domain-specific knowledge can be encoded into the RL reward.**Hierarchical Policy Optimization**tackles simultaneous speech translation of unbounded speech, using a hierarchical reward structure to guide LLM post-training.**ReaGeo**applies RL with a distance-deviation-based reward to optimize LLM-based geocoding accuracy, showing the versatility of the approach.**R2IF**introduces a composite reward for interpretable function calling, combining chain-of-thought effectiveness with structured output validation.**AEL**and**DiffMAS**also contribute here, with**DiffMAS**enabling end-to-end optimization of latent communication in multi-agent systems and**AEL** focusing on open-ended environments.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
