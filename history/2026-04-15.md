## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### Reinforcement Learning for LLMs
Today's research demonstrates a powerful convergence on using Reinforcement Learning (RL) as the primary engine for steering and improving LLM behavior, with a clear trend towards more sophisticated, efficient, and verifiable reward mechanisms. A key breakthrough is the move beyond simple outcome-based rewards to dense, process-oriented signals. Methods like **OOM-RL**introduce un-hackable, market-driven objectives, while**CPMI**and**MISE**focus on scalable, automatic labeling of step-level rewards and calibrated hindsight self-evaluation. To address the core challenge of credit assignment in long reasoning chains, novel techniques like**Entropy-Aware Policy Optimization (EAPO)**,**Rollout-Tree Monte Carlo (RTMC)**, and**SCOPE**'s signal-calibrated supervision are proposed. Furthermore, efficiency is a major concern, with**Low-rank Optimization Trajectories Modeling**aiming to accelerate RL training and**You Only Judge Once**enabling efficient multi-response reward modeling. The collective direction is towards RL frameworks that are more interpretable, stable, and capable of optimizing complex, multi-step behaviors like negotiation, tool-use, and structured generation.

### Self-Improving & Evolving Agents
The field is rapidly advancing towards fully autonomous systems capable of iterative self-improvement without human intervention. The paradigm is shifting from static agents to dynamic, learning entities that evolve through experience. Frameworks like**ReflectiChain**,**EE-MCP**, and**Mem$^2$Evolve**exemplify this by incorporating mechanisms for automated environment generation, experience banking, and co-evolutionary capability expansion. Crucially, this self-improvement is often driven by RL loops, as seen in**Camyla**for autonomous medical research and the**Pioneer Agent**system for continual model refinement in production. The ultimate test of this capability is benchmarked by**Agent^2 RL-Bench**, which evaluates whether LLM agents can autonomously engineer RL pipelines to improve foundation models. This direction points to a future where agents are not just tools, but resilient, adaptive systems that learn from interaction, diagnose failures, and curate their own training data in closed loops.

### Latent Reasoning & Computational Efficiency
A significant thrust aims to move explicit, verbose Chain-of-Thought reasoning into more compact, latent, and efficient computational forms. The goal is to preserve or enhance reasoning quality while drastically reducing memory and computational overhead. Architectures like**Looped Reasoning Language Models**and**Introspective Diffusion Language Models**fundamentally alter the model's computation graph to enable iterative refinement in a latent space. Complementary methods like**MEMENTO**teach LLMs to compress reasoning blocks into dense state summaries, and**ZoomR**and**CASK**propose sophisticated KV cache compression techniques that protect core reasoning traces. The theoretical underpinning is strengthened by work like**Transformers Learn Latent Mixture Models In-Context**, which reveals the latent computational mechanisms already present in attention layers. This collective effort seeks to unlock deeper, more efficient reasoning by internalizing the process, moving from token-level narrative to latent-state evolution.

### Multimodal & Embodied Reasoning
Research extends the RL and latent reasoning paradigms to multimodal and embodied domains, focusing on grounding model behavior in visual, physical, and interactive contexts. The core challenge is anchoring model decisions to sensory input to mitigate hallucination. Frameworks like**MedLVR**and**Visual Enhanced Depth Scaling**employ latent state refinement for visual question answering, while**V-STAR**uses hierarchical visual attention rewards to explicitly tether reasoning to pixels. In embodied settings,**OOWM**structures planning via object-oriented programmatic world models optimized with outcome-based RL, and**AIM**utilizes self-distilled RL for robot action generation. The trend is towards unified policies that can reason over and act within complex, multi-modal state spaces, using RL to align the agent's internal reasoning process with the external, verifiable constraints of the physical or visual world.

### Specialized RL Frameworks & Applications
Today's output includes a proliferation of highly specialized RL frameworks designed for niche but critical applications, demonstrating the versatility of RL as a fine-tuning tool. These methods often introduce novel reward designs to solve domain-specific ambiguity or optimization challenges. For instance,**CSPO**disentangles component-specific rewards for structured LaTeX generation,**ASPIRin**optimizes turn-taking in speech LMs via action space projection, and**Policy Split**uses dual-mode entropy regularization to encourage diverse exploration in open-ended tasks. Other works, like**HiEdit**for lifelong model editing and**PhyMix**'s Scene-GRPO for physically consistent 3D generation, showcase RL's ability to perform precise, adaptive control over model behavior. This specialization indicates a maturation of the field, where RL is no longer a blunt instrument but a precision tool for aligning LLMs with complex, real-world objectives.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
