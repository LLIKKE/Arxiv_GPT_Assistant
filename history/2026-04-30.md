## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs
A significant cluster of papers today pushes the boundaries of reinforcement learning for language model post-training, moving beyond standard RLHF and GRPO. A major theme is the formalization and treatment of reward design and optimization dynamics. **Frictive Policy Optimization (FPO)**introduces a framework where clarification and refusal are treated as epistemic control actions, directly addressing the nuanced problem of model uncertainty and risk-sensitive alignment. Complementing this, a theoretical paper categorizes reward errors as potentially benign or beneficial, providing a foundational lens for designing more robust reward models. On the optimization front, the**Tsallis loss continuum**is proposed to solve cold-start stalling in reasoning model RLVR, while**Compute Aligned Training**redefines the training objective to directly optimize for test-time inference strategies. Further innovations include a feature-level mechanistic study explaining why RL generalizes better than SFT, a reinforcement learning-based query refiner for eliciting reasoning from frozen models, and novel applications of RL to video generation (via GRPO), cross-lingual RAG, image editing (with verifiable checklists), tabular data cleaning, and machine translation. Finally,**RMiPO**introduces a hyperparameter-free offline preference optimization method using intrinsic mutual information, and a ranking-based reward model is proposed for explanation quality assessment.

### Self-Evolving Agents & Safety
The frontier of autonomous agent improvement is strongly represented, with a focus on closed-loop, self-referential architectures.**Escher-Loop**proposes a fully closed-loop framework for the mutual evolution of task and optimizer agents, achieving a pure self-evolving criterion. In a similar vein, the**Self-Synthesizing Reasoning Protocols (SSRP)**framework separates planning and execution for metacognitive self-improvement, while**Agentic Harness Engineering (AHE)**uses observability-driven loops to evolve coding-agent harnesses autonomously. For safety, a particularly notable paper,**Discovering Agentic Safety Specifications from 1-Bit Danger Signals**, proposes a framework (EPO-Safe) that enables agents to self-discover and refine their own safety specifications from sparse feedback, representing a critical step towards robust and scalable agentic safety. Finally,**ClawTrace**introduces a cost-aware tracing and distillation pipeline for efficient agent skill improvement from interaction traces.

### Latent Reasoning & Inference-Time Scaling
A clear trend is the deepening exploration of latent reasoning, moving beyond simple chain-of-thought into more sophisticated architectures and decoding strategies.**Ulterior Motives**directly tackles the safety implications of continuous thought models by introducing a benchmark and detection method for misaligned reasoning in latent space. On the architectural side,**Nautile-370M**proposes a hybrid model that integrates latent spectral memory with attention for efficient reasoning. For decoding,**Exploratory Sampling (ESamp)**uses a lightweight distiller to predict hidden representations and leverages prediction error as a novelty signal for semantic exploration, offering a new method for test-time scaling. This is further complemented by**One Refiner to Unlock Them All**, which uses RL to train a query refiner that elicits reasoning from frozen LLMs at inference time.

### Multimodal & Video Generation
Advances in multimodal reasoning and generation are driven by RL and novel latent reasoning frameworks. In video generation, a systematic post-training framework is proposed, incorporating RLHF with a novel GRPO method to optimize diffusion models. For image editing,**DDA-Thinker**introduces a decoupled dual-atomic RL framework that uses verifiable checklists to optimize a reasoning planning module. Hallucination mitigation in Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs) is addressed by**AVES-DPO**, which creates a self-corrected preference learning pipeline using consensus-based verification. Finally,**The Thinking Pixel** proposes a recursive sparse mixture-of-experts framework for latent reasoning directly within diffusion model latents, bridging the gap between language model reasoning and generative diffusion processes.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
