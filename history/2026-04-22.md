## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs & Alignment
Today's research demonstrates a concerted push to address the fundamental instabilities and inefficiencies of applying reinforcement learning to large language models. A key theme is the development of novel RL frameworks that move beyond standard policy gradients to ensure stable, monotonic improvement, as seen in methods like Bounded Ratio RL and Mastery-Consolidated Policy Optimization (MCPO). Concurrently, significant effort is directed at solving the exploration problem in sparse-reward reasoning tasks, with innovations like Poly-EPO for optimistic exploration and HEAL for mitigating entropy collapse. The community is also critically re-examining core RLHF components, proposing new frameworks for step-level reward modeling, addressing length bias in sequence-level comparisons, and even providing new theoretical grounding for the effectiveness of online alignment methods. This collective work signifies a maturation of the field, moving from initial proof-of-concepts to robust, theoretically-sound training paradigms.

### Self-Improving & Evolving Agents
The frontier of agent research is dominated by architectures designed for continuous, autonomous improvement without heavy human oversight. A clear trend is the formalization of self-evolution loops, where agents systematically externalize lessons from failures into reusable components—be they skill banks, optimized prompts, executable code, or refined hypotheses—as demonstrated by frameworks like EvoMaster, SkillFlow, and GenericAgent. This evolution is often driven by multi-agent ecosystems or self-play paradigms that generate rich, synthetic interaction data, such as in Agent-World's training arena or the collusive strategy evolution studied in prompt optimization. The underlying mechanisms are increasingly sophisticated, combining reinforcement learning with retrieval, code generation, and dynamic graph-based collaboration to enable agents that not only solve tasks but perpetually enhance their own capabilities and internal knowledge structures.

### Latent & Efficient Reasoning
A major thrust aims to compress and accelerate the multi-step reasoning processes of LLMs by operating in latent or compressed spaces. The goal is to achieve the benefits of chain-of-thought with the efficiency of single-step generation. Techniques like LEPO apply RL directly to continuous latent representations, while OneVL and Semantic Step Prediction forecast multi-step reasoning trajectories into compact latent plans. Efficiency is pursued hand-in-hand with this latent reasoning, through dynamic KV-cache management (Neural Garbage Collection), low-rank activation compression (LASER), and internalizing early-exit mechanisms (Step-GRPO). This line of work seeks a fundamental shift from expensive, explicit token-by-token reasoning to efficient, implicit computational processes that maintain or improve performance.

### Multimodal & Embodied Agents
Research in multimodal and embodied domains is increasingly focused on verifiable, grounded learning and the challenge of continual skill acquisition. For Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), frameworks like EVE emphasize verifiable self-evolution through executable visual transformations, ensuring improvements are grounded in reality. In embodied AI, the focus is on creating automated, lifelong learning pipelines, as seen in DexWorldModel's online framework for learning from a stream of physics-grounded trajectories. The integration of reinforcement learning with verifiable, often simulation-based, reward signals is a common thread, driving systems that can interact with and improve from complex, non-textual environments.

### Federated & Collaborative Learning
There is growing interest in decentralized and collaborative paradigms for training and improving AI systems. Federation over Text proposes a shared library where agents distill and share reasoning traces, enabling collective intelligence. On the technical front, new algorithms like federated RLHF via zeroth-order optimization aim to make these collaborative training processes scalable and privacy-preserving. This direction points towards a future where improvement is not siloed within single models or institutions but emerges from secure, distributed collaboration and knowledge exchange among multiple agents or entities.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
