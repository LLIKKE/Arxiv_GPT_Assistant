# Output Instructions

You must respond ONLY with a valid JSONL (JSON Lines) format. Do not include any introductory or concluding text, explanations, or markdown formatting (e.g., do not wrap in ```jsonl). 

Each line of your response must be a single, valid JSON object representing one paper, containing exactly the following keys:
- "ARXIVID": (string) The ArXiv ID of the paper.
- "RELEVANCE": (boolean) Set to `true` ONLY if the paper strongly and directly matches at least one research criterion as a central contribution. If the match is partial, adjacent, generic, application-focused, benchmark-only, or ambiguous, set it to `false`.
- "RELEVANCE_SCORE": (integer) A score from 0 to 10 evaluating how relevant the paper is to the criteria. Be strict and mean in scoring. The distribution should be conservative: most papers should fall in 0-5, only clearly strong matches should get 6-7, and scores 8-10 should be rare. Use the following guide:
  - 0: Completely irrelevant.
  - 1-2: Bare keyword overlap or very distant connection.
  - 3-4: Tangentially related, but not about the target problem itself.
  - 5: Borderline / marginal relevance; touches the area but should usually not be selected.
  - 6: Relevant in a meaningful way, but not a strong fit.
  - 7: Clearly relevant and useful, with a direct connection to the target topic.
  - 8: Strong match; one of the paper's main contributions is tightly aligned.
  - 9: Exceptional match; highly important for the target topic.
  - 10: Near-perfect match; must-read paper with unusually strong alignment.
- "NOVELTY_SCORE": (integer) A score from 0 to 10 evaluating the novelty and originality of the paper's core contribution. The score MUST have good differentiation. Use the following guide:
  - 0-4: Incremental work, straightforward application of existing methods, or very minor tweaks.
  - 5: Slight novelty (minor variations of known architectures/methods).
  - 6: Moderate novelty (some interesting new components or combinations).
  - 7: Good novelty (clear new approach or significant improvement over baselines).
  - 8: High novelty (very original idea, introduces a new paradigm or solves a hard problem).
  - 9: Extremely high novelty (groundbreaking, opens up a completely new research direction).
  - 10: Transformative (rare, paradigm-shifting paper).
- "COMMENT": (string) A concise 1-sentence summary explaining WHY this paper is relevant and highlighting its core contribution. (If RELEVANCE is false, this can be an empty string).

Additional scoring rules:
- Use high scores sparingly. A paper should not receive `RELEVANCE_SCORE >= 8` unless it is directly centered on one of the target topics.
- If unsure between two scores, choose the lower one.
