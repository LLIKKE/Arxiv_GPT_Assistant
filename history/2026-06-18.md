## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### On-Policy Distillation for LLMs
A clear trend emerges with multiple papers advancing on-policy distillation (OPD) as a core methodology for improving LLMs. The field is moving beyond simple imitation learning toward more sophisticated techniques that address the fundamental training-inference mismatch. PowerOPD introduces a bounded power-transformation reward that stabilizes training dynamics and boosts reasoning accuracy over vanilla OPD. Zone of Proximal Policy Optimization (ZPPO) takes a novel approach by keeping the teacher in the prompt rather than the gradient, reformulating prompts to guide student learning without direct gradient interference. For diffusion language models, Learning from the Self-future (d-OPSD) provides the first on-policy self-distillation framework with step-level supervision, while Self-Generated Error Training enables token editors to fix their own draft errors. The quality-aware self-distillation method for GUI grounding demonstrates how correctness-aware gating can improve teacher signal quality. These works collectively suggest that OPD is maturing into a family of principled techniques that can stabilize training, improve sample efficiency, and enable continuous self-improvement without external supervision.

### Reinforcement Learning for Reasoning and Efficiency
Reinforcement learning continues to drive breakthroughs in LLM reasoning, with several papers tackling fundamental limitations of current approaches. Dynamic Epistemic Entropy Orchestrated Erasable Reinforcement Learning (E3RL) directly confronts the autoregressive curse by using dynamic entropy thresholds to enable self-healing reasoning—excising early logical defects and reusing KV cache for long-horizon tasks. SuCo introduces a two-stage framework combining alignment fine-tuning with sufficiency-aware policy optimization to dynamically control reasoning length, directly targeting the efficiency-reliability trade-off. Dynamic Rollout Editing (DRE) addresses the overthinking problem in RL-trained models by editing rollout trajectories during GRPO-style training to improve credit assignment. Rethinking Groups in Critic-Free RLVR proposes negative token filtering for stable single-rollout training, eliminating data inefficiency from group synchronization. The theoretical framework in From Reasoning Traces to Reusable Modules provides crucial foundations, showing how SFT and RL play complementary roles in compositional generalization. These advances collectively indicate a shift toward more adaptive, efficient, and theoretically grounded RL methods that can handle the unique challenges of language model reasoning.

### Latent Reasoning and Deep Looped Architectures
A emerging paradigm focuses on latent reasoning within transformer architectures, moving beyond explicit chain-of-thought. LoopCoder-v2 introduces a parallel loop transformer for latent reasoning, carefully studying the gain-cost trade-off of loop count and achieving strong code reasoning results. Fixed-Point Reasoners propose a looped transformer with guaranteed fixed-point convergence as an end-to-end halting mechanism, directly addressing signal propagation challenges in deep looped architectures. Learning to Refine Hidden States (ReLAR) uses reinforcement-guided latent refinement to iteratively update hidden representations before decoding, applying policy gradients at the representation level rather than token level. These approaches share a common insight: reasoning can happen in the latent space of transformer layers, potentially offering more efficient and controllable computation than autoregressive token generation. The convergence guarantees and adaptive halting mechanisms suggest this direction is moving toward practical, stable implementations.

### Multimodal and Agentic Systems
The integration of RL with multimodal and agentic systems shows significant progress. See First, Answer Later introduces sufficiency-driven RL (via GRPO) for visual evidence pre-alignment in MLLMs, directly improving visual grounding and reasoning by ensuring models attend to relevant visual evidence before generating answers. MaineCoon pushes toward real-time audio-visual world models using reinforced online-policy distillation (ROPD) as a core technique, demonstrating how OPD can be adapted for multimodal autoregressive models. STAR provides spatiotemporally adaptive reward allocation for text-to-image RL post-training, using attention maps to focus policy updates on relevant latent regions—a critical advance for diffusion model fine-tuning. On the agentic front, EnvRL augments agentic RL with auxiliary environment dynamics learning objectives (state prediction and inverse dynamics), showing clear improvements on long-horizon tasks by helping models understand environment transitions. OPD-Evolver proposes a slow-fast co-evolution framework using on-policy self-distillation to cultivate holistic agent evolvers, suggesting that OPD can serve as a unifying mechanism for agent improvement across multiple dimensions.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, scalable self-improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures with clear technical substance.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## 3. On-Policy Distillation for LLMs
We care about distillation methods that improve LLM capabilities by training on the model's own on-policy outputs, trajectories, preferences, corrections, or interaction data, especially when the student distribution is actively refreshed during training.
- **Strong match:** On-policy distillation, self-distillation from sampled rollouts, online teacher-student distillation, policy-improvement distillation, iterative distillation from model-generated reasoning traces, distillation from verifier/reward-selected on-policy samples, and methods that close the gap between training data distribution and the model's current behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new iterative distillation loop, sampling-and-selection strategy, online data refresh mechanism, teacher/student update schedule, or objective that measurably improves reasoning, instruction-following, alignment, tool use, or general LLM capability.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard offline knowledge distillation, static SFT on teacher-generated data, model compression only, data cleaning only, speculative decoding/distillation for speed only, or generic synthetic data generation without an on-policy or iterative policy-improvement component.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
