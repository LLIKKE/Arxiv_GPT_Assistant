## 💡 Daily Research Summary
<div style='background-color: #f6f8fa; border-left: 4px solid #24292e; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px;'>
### RL for LLMs
The dominant theme today is the rapid maturation of Reinforcement Learning for Large Language Models (RL4LLMs), moving beyond simple outcome-based rewards to sophisticated, multi-faceted training paradigms. A significant cluster of papers focuses on enhancing process supervision and reward design. For instance, **GRPO-VPS**introduces verifiable process supervision via belief probing to improve reasoning accuracy, while**V-tableR1**proposes a critic-guided policy optimization with step-level feedback for multimodal reasoning.**Rethinking Reinforcement Fine-Tuning in LVLM**provides crucial theoretical grounding, establishing convergence and generalization bounds for RL fine-tuning with verifiable rewards. Furthermore, novel optimization strategies are emerging:**Near-Future Policy Optimization (NPO)**accelerates convergence by using a policy's own later checkpoint as an auxiliary trajectory source, and**Avoiding Overthinking and Underthinking**introduces a curriculum-aware budget scheduling framework. Critically, the field is also exploring how to shape LLM behavior through reward structure, as seen in**The Tool-Overuse Illusion**, which analyzes and mitigates tool overuse by aligning reward structures with epistemic boundaries. This wave of research demonstrates a clear shift toward more granular, theoretically sound, and behaviorally nuanced RL training recipes.

### Agents & Self-Improving Systems
A parallel and deeply interconnected trend is the evolution of agents from static tools into self-improving systems that learn from their own trajectories. Several papers propose frameworks where agents meta-learn to optimize their own configurations.**Learning to Evolve**introduces a meta-learning approach to optimize a textual parameter graph for multi-agent systems, while**WorkflowGen**generates adaptive workflows from trajectory experience in a closed-loop manner. The concept of "self-play" is being directly applied to agentic learning:**Bootstrapping Post-training Signals for Open-ended Tasks**uses rubric-based self-play on pretraining text to generate training signals, and**Self-Guided Self-Play (SGS)**for theorem proving guides a model away from degeneracy. Practical applications are also prominent, with**DR-Venus**demonstrating that frontier-grade deep research agents can be trained from only 10K open data points using agentic SFT and RL, and**ProMMSearchAgent**using a sim-to-real paradigm with process-oriented rewards. This convergence indicates that the next frontier for agent research is not just building capable systems, but creating the training methodologies that allow them to autonomously refine their skills, strategies, and even their own internal structures.

### Multimodal Reasoning & Post-Training
The application of RL to multimodal models is rapidly expanding, with a strong focus on visual reasoning and grounding.**SSL-R1**breaks new ground by deriving verifiable rewards directly from images for self-supervised visual RL post-training, offering a scalable alternative to human annotation.**Hybrid Latent Reasoning (HyLaR)**addresses the challenge of reasoning in discrete-continuous action spaces, which is fundamental for embodied agents. For more specific domains,**ToolsRL**provides a framework with tool-specific rewards for visual reasoning, and**Video-ToC**introduces a reasoning-demand reward mechanism for video understanding. Even specialized clinical applications are benefiting, as**Infection-Reasoner**uses GRPO post-training to refine evidence-grounded reasoning for wound classification. This body of work shows that the principles of RL4LLMs are being successfully adapted to the multimodal domain, creating powerful feedback loops that improve both visual perception and the chain-of-thought reasoning built upon it.

### Reasoning & Alignment
Beyond general RL, there is a focused effort to refine the reasoning process itself, addressing persistent issues like logical consistency, overthinking, and alignment with user intent.**Where Reasoning Breaks**proposes a surgical RL objective that selectively optimizes token-level preferences at critical logical pivots, moving beyond whole-sequence optimization.**HiPO**introduces hierarchical preference optimization for segment-level reasoning alignment, offering a more granular approach than standard DPO. For instruction following,**Self-Guided Plan Extraction**uses goal-conditional RL to jointly train planners and agents. The intersection of reasoning and tool use is also being explored:**R2IF**aligns reasoning with decisions via composite rewards for interpretable function calling, and**JTPRO**iteratively co-optimizes instructions and tool schemas from rollout traces. Finally,**Differentiable Conformal Training** offers a novel approach to improving reasoning factuality by learning a scorer that optimizes for claim retention under reliability guarantees, a promising direction for open-ended generation. These papers collectively illustrate a move toward more controlled, logical, and faithful reasoning in LLMs, driven by finely-tuned RL signals.

# Research Interest Criteria

Please evaluate papers using a strict filter. We only want a small set of strongly aligned papers, not a broad survey of adjacent work.

## Global Selection Principle
- Mark a paper as relevant only if one of the three topics below is a central contribution of the paper, not a side application, benchmark, or discussion point.
- Be conservative by default: if the connection is indirect, partial, or ambiguous, treat it as not relevant or score it low.
- Prefer papers that introduce new methods, training frameworks, reward designs, agent improvement loops, or latent reasoning architectures.
- Do not favor papers just because they mention "LLM", "agent", "reasoning", "RL", "alignment", or "CoT" in a generic way.
- Exclude broad foundation model papers, generic post-training papers, generic tool-use agents, application/system papers, domain-specific deployments, and pure benchmark papers unless their core method strongly matches one of the topics below.

## 1. Reinforcement Learning (RL) for LLMs
We care about RL methods that directly improve LLM behavior, alignment, or reasoning through explicit reward design, RL pipelines, verifier-driven optimization, or scalable preference/feedback mechanisms.
- **Strong match:** RLHF/RLAIF variants, verifiable reward based RL, rejection sampling plus RL pipelines, reward model design, outcome/process reward methods, online RL for LLMs, and methods that materially improve helpfulness, honesty, harmlessness, or hallucination behavior.
- **Especially valuable:** Papers that propose a new reward function, a new scalable RL training recipe, or a new data-generation / self-improvement loop for post-training LLMs.
- **Usually not relevant:** Generic reinforcement learning papers, RL for robotics/control/games, prompt optimization without RL, supervised finetuning only, inference-time search without a training contribution, or alignment papers with no substantive RL component.

## 2. Agents for LLMs
We care specifically about self-evolving agents rather than static agent systems. The central question is whether the agent can improve itself over time through interaction, reflection, tool-use traces, self-generated supervision, or automated curriculum/data generation.
- **Strong match:** Self-improving agent frameworks, reflection/self-critique loops that feed back into learning, trajectory-driven continual improvement, automatic tool-use policy improvement, environment interaction that produces new supervision, and long-horizon agent adaptation.
- **Especially valuable:** Closed-loop systems showing measurable capability gain over time, reliable agent learning from interaction traces, or agent architectures that turn execution experience into better future performance.
- **Usually not relevant:** Vanilla ReAct-style agents, workflow orchestration, multi-agent communication only, tool calling systems without learning, benchmark papers, memory systems without self-improvement, or application-focused agent papers.

## 3. Latent CoT/Reasoning
We care about methods that move reasoning away from verbose explicit text chains into compact latent computation while preserving or improving reasoning quality.
- **Strong match:** Latent reasoning architectures, hidden-state reasoning, implicit CoT, recurrent depth / recurrent latent computation, pause or thinking tokens for internal computation, explicit-to-latent distillation, and methods that reduce reasoning token cost or KV-cache use while retaining accuracy.
- **Especially valuable:** New architectures or objectives for latent reasoning, hidden-state alignment/distillation, compression of reasoning traces, or methods that materially improve reasoning efficiency on math and logic tasks.
- **Usually not relevant:** Standard explicit CoT prompting, generic reasoning finetuning, test-time scaling without latent computation, reasoning benchmarks, or papers whose main contribution is evaluation rather than a latent reasoning mechanism.

## Final Preference
If a paper is merely adjacent to these areas, do not select it. Favor precision over recall. It is better to miss a borderline paper than to include many weakly related ones.
